About Me

I am a computational atmospheric scientist with an engineering background, specializing in high-performance computing (HPC)-scale numerical cloud modeling. My primary focus is on Large Eddy Simulation (LES) with the Super-Droplet Method (SDM), enabling detailed exploration of cloud microphysics and their role in severe weather and climate. Currently, I serve as an Assistant Professor at the University of Hyogo, actively contributing to the Shima Research Group's efforts under Moonshot Project Goal 8, which aims to advance severe weather control technologies. My research involves running SDM-LES simulations on the Fugaku supercomputer, often executing large-scale CPU jobs across tens of thousands of nodes. My expertise spans algorithm design, performance tuning, and running large-scale simulations, balancing microphysical fidelity, computational performance, and operational readiness is central to my work. I am also passionate about integrating data-driven and AI-assisted approaches to further accelerate scientific workflows and model development.

Lab: Shima Research Group (University of Hyogo)

Outside the lab: drip coffee, technology, cinema, cultural traditions, and photography.
